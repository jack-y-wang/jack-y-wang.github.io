Welcome to CS61A!

Here are some tips and advice for studying for CS 61A


Ask for Help and go to OH
  • If you are having a hard time wrapping your head around a topic and understanding it. Don't be afraid to seek out help. There are great resources on campus to seek help from. These include:
    • CS61A Course Staff
    • Computer Science Mentors (CSM)
    • CS 370 Student Tutoring
    • Eta Kappa Nu (HKN) Tutoring Hours
    • Upsilon Pi Epsilon (UPE) Office Hours
    • Computer Science Undergraduate Association (CSUA) Office Hours
  • Students in these organizations great understanding of the concepts covered in CS61A, and have went through the process and experience of taking the class. So they are great resource for relevant and personal help and advice from. And of course ask peers your labs and discussions, and friends who are taking the class as well.
Create handwritten study sheats/study guides
  • This is not the most time efficient method for studying. But for me I found that I study best when I create handwritten review sheets on each topic going over my notes, slides, lectures, and other reference material. If you find yourself struggling in this class or other classes I think this may be worth a shot to try, but please manage your time though
Quality > Quantity
  • I know this is a phrase has kind of become cliche. But I think when it comes to studying for the midterms and final, I recommend not creating an objective of going through as maney practice and previous tests as you can but rather simply do a couple of the most recent or most relevant (ie tests written by your current professor) tests and really try to understand the thought process and steps needed for each question on them. I find this as much more beneficial, and I think just doing a couple will give you enough experience with the types of questions you may encounter on exams
WWPD and Environment Diagrams
  • I think these types of questions tend to be much more systematic requiring just slowling walking through the problem and remembering the rules and order of operations of the program. I recommend watching the midterm and final walkthrough solution videos on these problems to get an idea of the methodical apporach to these problems.
  • If you are short on time for studying for the midterms, I recommend focusing on studying for this and trying to get as much points as possible
