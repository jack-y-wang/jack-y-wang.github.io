Filter Bubbles: Twitter

Image from MIT Medial Lab: Twitter Political Filter Bubbles

In my college writing class centered on the effects of technology and the digital age on society, we read and discussed papers from Safiya Noble on algorithmic bias and a hegemonic culture, readings about privacy and security by women from Deep Lab in Carnegie Mellong University, and read about misuses of data models in Weapons of Math Destruction by Cathy O'Neil.

For my final research paper in the class, I analyzed the effects social media, particularly Twitter, has on political polarization and creating an "us vs. them" environment. I discussed factors such as homophilic friend networks, filter bubbles, bots, and people's own biases being amplified.

If you'd like to discuss anything relating to technologies effects on society, positive or negative, feel free to send me a message at jack.y.wang@berkeley.edu
